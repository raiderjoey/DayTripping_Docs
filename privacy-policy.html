Privacy Policy for DayTripping

Last updated: June 19, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Waltco (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, and shares information when you use the DayTripping mobile application (the “App”). DayTripping is a collaborative trip-planning app that lets you and the people you travel with organize trips, itineraries, proposals, and packing lists.

By using DayTripping, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Who we are

DayTripping is published by Waltco.

For any privacy question or request, contact us at:

Email: waltcoappdev@gmail.com

We are the data controller for the personal information described below.

2. Information we collect

We only collect information that is needed to operate the App and to keep it stable. We do not use advertising networks or analytics SDKs, and we do not sell your data.

a. Account information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Email address — used as your login identifier and shown to other members of trips you join.
  • Password — handled and stored in hashed form by our authentication provider (Google Firebase Authentication). We never see or store your password in plain text.

We do not collect your name, photo, phone number, date of birth, or any demographic information at sign-up.

b. Content you create

When you use the App, you enter and store content such as:

  • Trip names, destinations, and travel dates.
  • Lodging details (e.g. accommodation name, address, check-in/out dates, confirmation numbers).
  • Transport details (e.g. carrier, origin, destination, times, confirmation numbers).
  • Activities and meals, including optional titles, places, times, addresses, phone numbers, web links, and free-text notes.
  • Proposals you send to other trip members, and your accept/decline responses.
  • Packing lists and outfit plans.

This content is entered manually by you. The App does not access your device’s GPS or location services; any addresses are typed in by you, not detected automatically.

c. Push notification token

If you enable push notifications, we store a device push token provided by the Expo push service so we can notify you about trip activity (for example, when someone sends you a proposal or responds to one). You can disable notifications at any time in your device settings.

d. Crash and diagnostic data

To keep the App stable, we use Firebase Crashlytics (a Google service) to collect crash reports automatically when the App stops working unexpectedly. A crash report may include:

  • Diagnostic details about the crash, such as the type of error and a stack trace.
  • Device and app state at the time of the crash, such as device model, operating system version, and app version.
  • An opaque user identifier (your account’s internal ID) so we can tell whether a crash affected one user or many. This identifier is not your email and does not directly identify you; it is cleared when you sign out.

Crash reports do not include the content of your trips, itineraries, proposals, or packing lists. Crash collection is disabled during development and only operates in released versions of the App.

e. On-device cache

The App stores a copy of your trip data locally on your device so it can work offline. This cache is cleared when you sign out.

3. Device permissions

DayTripping requests only one device permission:

  • Notifications — to deliver trip-related push notifications. This is optional and you can decline or revoke it at any time.

The App does not request access to your location, camera, microphone, contacts, photos, or calendar.

4. How we use your information

We use the information described above to:

  • Create and manage your account and keep you signed in.
  • Provide the core features of the App (trips, itineraries, proposals, packing lists).
  • Share trip content with the people you choose to plan trips with.
  • Send you push notifications about activity in your trips (if enabled).
  • Diagnose crashes and fix bugs so the App stays stable and reliable.
  • Maintain the security and integrity of the service.

We do not use your information for advertising, profiling, or automated decision-making.

5. How information is shared with other users

DayTripping is a collaborative app, so some information is shared with other members of the trips you participate in. Within a shared trip:

  • Visible to all members of that trip: your email address, the trip name and dates, destinations, shared lodging and transport details, and any proposals you create (including their addresses, phone numbers, links, and notes) along with each member’s accept/decline status.
  • Private to you only (not visible to other members): your personal itinerary items, your packing lists and outfit plans, and your push notification token.

Anyone you give a trip join code to can use that code to join the trip and will then see the shared information above. Only share join codes with people you trust.

6. Third-party service providers

We rely on a small number of service providers (“sub-processors”) to run the App. We share information with them only as needed to provide the service:

  • Google Firebase (Google LLC) — provides authentication, the cloud database where your account and trip content are stored, the cloud functions that send notifications, and Firebase Crashlytics for crash reporting (see Section 2d). See Google’s Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy and Firebase’s privacy and security information: https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy.
  • Expo push notification service (Expo / 650 Industries, Inc.) — delivers push notifications to your device. See: https://expo.dev/privacy.

These providers process your information on our behalf and are not permitted to use it for their own purposes beyond providing their service.

We do not integrate any analytics or advertising SDKs.

7. Where your data is stored

Your account and trip content are stored on Google Firebase’s cloud infrastructure. This means your information may be processed and stored on servers located outside your country of residence, including in the United States. Where required by law, transfers of personal data are protected by appropriate safeguards provided by our service providers.

8. Data retention

We retain your account information and trip content for as long as your account is active. If you ask us to delete your account, we will delete your account and associated personal data as described in Section 9.

Note that content you contributed to a shared trip (such as proposals or shared lodging/transport details) may remain visible to other members of that trip after your account is deleted, because it forms part of their trip records.

9. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information, and to object to or restrict certain processing.

Account and data deletion: To request deletion of your account and the personal data associated with it, email us at waltcoappdev@gmail.com from the email address associated with your account, with the subject line “Delete my account”. We will process your request within 30 days.

To exercise any other privacy right, contact us at the same address. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

10. Security

We use industry-standard measures provided by our infrastructure providers to protect your information, including encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) and database access rules that restrict who can read and write your data. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

We do not verify ownership of email addresses at sign-up, so please use an email address you control.

11. Children’s privacy

DayTripping is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at waltcoappdev@gmail.com and we will delete it.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of this page. Significant changes may be communicated through the App. Your continued use of the App after an update means you accept the revised policy.
