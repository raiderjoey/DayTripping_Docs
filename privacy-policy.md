# Privacy Policy for DayTripping

**Last updated: June 19, 2026**

This Privacy Policy explains how Waltco ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses,
and shares information when you use the **DayTripping** mobile application (the
"App"). DayTripping is a collaborative trip-planning app that lets you and the
people you travel with organize trips, itineraries, proposals, and packing
lists.

By using DayTripping, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If
you do not agree, please do not use the App.

---

DayTripping is published by **Waltco**.

For any privacy question or request, contact us at:

**Email: waltcoappdev@gmail.com**

## 2. Information we collect

We only collect information that is needed to operate the App and to keep it
stable. We do **not** use advertising networks or analytics SDKs, and we do
**not** sell your data.

### a. Account information
When you create an account, we collect:

- **Email address** — used as your login identifier and shown to other members
  of trips you join.
- **Password** — handled and stored in hashed form by our authentication
  provider (Google Firebase Authentication). We never see or store your
  password in plain text.

We do **not** collect your name, photo, phone number, date of birth, or any
demographic information at sign-up.

### b. Content you create
When you use the App, you enter and store content such as:

- Trip names, destinations, and travel dates.
- Lodging details (e.g. accommodation name, address, check-in/out dates,
  confirmation numbers).
- Transport details (e.g. carrier, origin, destination, times, confirmation
  numbers).
- Activities and meals, including optional titles, places, times, addresses,
  phone numbers, web links, and free-text notes.
- Proposals you send to other trip members, and your accept/decline responses.
- Packing lists and outfit plans.

This content is entered **manually by you**. The App does **not** access your
device's GPS or location services; any addresses are typed in by you, not
detected automatically.

### c. Push notification token
If you enable push notifications, we store a **device push token** provided by
the Expo push service so we can notify you about trip activity (for example,
when someone sends you a proposal or responds to one). You can disable
notifications at any time in your device settings.

### d. Crash and diagnostic data
To keep the App stable, we use **Firebase Crashlytics** (a Google service) to
collect crash reports automatically when the App stops working unexpectedly. A
crash report may include:

- Diagnostic details about the crash, such as the type of error and a stack
  trace.
- Device and app state at the time of the crash, such as device model, operating
  system version, and app version.
- An **opaque user identifier** (your account's internal ID) so we can tell
  whether a crash affected one user or many. This identifier is not your email
  and does not directly identify you; it is cleared when you sign out.

Crash reports do **not** include the content of your trips, itineraries,
proposals, or packing lists. Crash collection is **disabled during development**
and only operates in released versions of the App.

### e. On-device cache
The App stores a copy of your trip data locally on your device so it can work
offline. This cache is cleared when you sign out.

## 3. Device permissions

DayTripping requests only one device permission:

- **Notifications** — to deliver trip-related push notifications. This is
  optional and you can decline or revoke it at any time.

The App does **not** request access to your location, camera, microphone,
contacts, photos, or calendar.

---

## 4. How we use your information

We use the information described above to:

- Create and manage your account and keep you signed in.
- Provide the core features of the App (trips, itineraries, proposals, packing
  lists).
- Share trip content with the people you choose to plan trips with.
- Send you push notifications about activity in your trips (if enabled).
- Diagnose crashes and fix bugs so the App stays stable and reliable.
- Maintain the security and integrity of the service.

We do **not** use your information for advertising, profiling, or automated
decision-making.

---

## 5. How information is shared with other users

DayTripping is a collaborative app, so some information is shared with other
members of the trips you participate in. Within a shared trip:

- **Visible to all members of that trip:** your email address, the trip name and
  dates, destinations, shared lodging and transport details, and any
  **proposals** you create (including their addresses, phone numbers, links, and
  notes) along with each member's accept/decline status.
- **Private to you only (not visible to other members):** your personal
  itinerary items, your packing lists and outfit plans, and your push
  notification token.

Anyone you give a trip **join code** to can use that code to join the trip and
will then see the shared information above. Only share join codes with people
you trust.

## 6. Third-party service providers

We rely on a small number of service providers ("sub-processors") to run the
App. We share information with them only as needed to provide the service:

- **Google Firebase** (Google LLC) — provides authentication, the cloud database
  where your account and trip content are stored, the cloud functions that send
  notifications, and **Firebase Crashlytics** for crash reporting (see Section
  2d). See Google's Privacy Policy: <https://policies.google.com/privacy> and
  Firebase's privacy and security information:
  <https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy>.
- **Expo push notification service** (Expo / 650 Industries, Inc.) — delivers
  push notifications to your device. See:
  <https://expo.dev/privacy>.

These providers process your information on our behalf and are not permitted to
use it for their own purposes beyond providing their service.

We do **not** integrate any analytics or advertising SDKs.

## 7. Where your data is stored

Your account and trip content are stored on Google Firebase's cloud
infrastructure. This means your information may be processed and stored on
servers located outside your country of residence, including in the United
States. Where required by law, transfers of personal data are protected by
appropriate safeguards provided by our service providers.

---

## 8. Data retention

We retain your account information and trip content for as long as your account
is active. If you ask us to delete your account, we will delete your account and
associated personal data as described in Section 9.

Note that content you contributed to a **shared trip** (such as proposals or
shared lodging/transport details) may remain visible to other members of that
trip after your account is deleted, because it forms part of their trip records.

## 10. Security

We use industry-standard measures provided by our infrastructure providers to
protect your information, including encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) and
database access rules that restrict who can read and write your data. No method

security.

We do not verify ownership of email addresses at sign-up, so please use an email
address you control.
